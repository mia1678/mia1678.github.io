Accessibility Research

The technology is improving, and this generation is the generation of internet. There are millions of documents, sources, information, knowledge, employment and many other things that people can search and reach online, and this makes Web Accessibility important since millions of people who have disabilities can be affected (W3C).

Web Accessibility provides equal access and equal opportunity for people even if they have certain disabilities, which means that people with disabilities can use the web by allowing them perceive, understand, navigate, and interact with the website and therefore contribute back to the website. Not only those people with disabilities will be benefited from web accessibilities, but those who have changing abilities due to aging, those who have slow internet connection, and those who have temporary disabilities can can gain. (W3C)

Web Accessibility benefits business since it easily allows people do business with them even though they might have certain disabilities like they have certain difficulty going out or they are blind or color blindness. In addition, according to W3C, accessibility is required by law in some cases which means making Web Accessibility can avoid being against the law (DailyTekk).

According to Government Technology and National Federation of the Blind, there are 20% of the population have certain disabilities and around 5 percent of them is visually impaired, there are also more than 2 percent of Americans have severe limitations to their dexterity (Government Technology and National Federation of the Blind). This indicates that if the website is not Web Accessibility, around 20% of the population can not reach to your website, this can harm the company since some of these people may want to do business with them but were hindered due to the fact that they cannot access the web page. Moreover, doing Web Accessibility can gain several positive reputation such as loyalty and trust from their customer, and this also indicated that this company cares and respects about people regardless who they are (Grant).
